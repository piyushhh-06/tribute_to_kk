Tribute to KK

1968-2022

KK is a renowned Indian playback singer. He was born on August 23, 1968, in Delhi, India. His real name is Krishnakumar Kunnath, but he is popularly known as KK. With his mesmerizing voice, KK has sung numerous hit songs in various languages including Hindi, Telugu, Tamil, Kannada, and Malayalam.

KK started his career in the 1990s and gained widespread recognition with his breakthrough song "Tadap Tadap" from the movie "Hum Dil De Chuke Sanam." Since then, he has delivered many chart-topping tracks and has collaborated with several music composers and directors in the Indian film industry.

Some of KK's notable songs include "Pal" from the movie "Kites," "Zara Sa" from the movie "Jannat," "Tune Maari Entriyaan" from the movie "Gunday," and "Awarapan Banjarapan" from the movie "Jism." His soulful voice and versatility have made him a favorite among music lovers.

KK has received several awards for his exceptional contribution to the music industry. He has won the Filmfare Award for Best Male Playback Singer and the IIFA Award for Best Male Playback, among others.

KK's passion for music continues to inspire aspiring singers and his fans. He has left an indelible mark on Indian music with his incredible talent and dedication. KK's melodious voice and emotional renditions have made him one of the most beloved singers in the country.

Let us celebrate the extraordinary talent of KK and his unforgettable contributions to the world of music.
